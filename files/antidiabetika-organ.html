Antidiabetika nach Wirkort

Master diagram of antidiabetic drug classes grouped by site of action, with key clinical data for each.

How to read this. Drugs are grouped by the organ they act on — pancreas, liver & periphery, kidney, gut, brain, and direct replacement. Every card uses the same 5-slot metric strip (A1c reduction · Wt weight · Hypo hypoglycemia risk · CV cardiovascular effect · K renal effect). Green means benefit, red means risk or avoid. The pearl is the one line a clinician should remember.

When endogenous production fails

Insulin

Pearl. Required in T1D, advanced T2D, pregnancy, hospitalized patients. Degludec and glargine U300 have the flattest profile and lowest nocturnal hypoglycemia. Basal-first intensification; add prandial if postprandial targets missed. Patient education and SMBG/CGM are the intervention, not the needle.
Pipeline & honorable mentions. Orforglipron (oral small-molecule GLP-1 RA), retatrutide (triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon), survodutide (GLP-1/glucagon), and cagrilintide + semaglutide (CagriSema) are in late-stage trials — not in this map because none are approved yet. Fixed-dose combinations (metformin + gliptin, basal + GLP-1 RA, etc.) are formulations, not classes.
